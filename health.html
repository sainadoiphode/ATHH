Badminton
Quick direction changes · Overhead swings · Jumps
Shoulder & elbow load Knee & ankle joints Single-leg landings
Shoulder overload (smash / clears)
Overuse

Dull ache or pinch at the front or top of shoulder, especially after many smashes and clears.

Green: mild post-session soreness Details ▼

Common when: Technique uses only arm instead of full body; no warm-up; sudden jump in volume.

Warning signs (red-flag side): Pain while simply lifting the arm overhead, pain at night, weakness when holding the racket, sharp pinch on every smash.

Immediate care: Cut down heavy smashes for a few days, apply ice 10–15 min after play, gentle pain-free range of motion. If pain > 1 week or getting worse, see a physio or sports doctor.

Prevention / future: Add basic band/weight work for rotator cuff and upper back, always warm-up with shadow swings first, progress jump-smash volume slowly instead of suddenly.

Tennis elbow (backhand grip stress)
Overuse

Pain at outer elbow when gripping, lifting the racket or hitting backhands.

Green: only mild after heavy session Details ▼

Common when: Very tight grip, wrong backhand technique, suddenly long sessions after a break.

Warning signs: Pain even when lifting light objects, gripping a bottle, or twisting a door handle; tenderness if you press on the outer elbow bone.

Immediate care: Reduce racket load for some time, use ice massage around the sore area, avoid playing through sharp pain. Consider an elbow strap only after consulting a professional.

Prevention / future: Relax your grip, improve technique, add simple forearm strengthening (wrist curls, extensions) with light weights or bands.

Ankle sprain (sideways lunge)
Acute

Sudden twist with immediate pain and swelling around ankle after landing or cutting sideways.

Pink: cannot put weight = stop play Details ▼

Common when: Court is slippery, shoes have poor grip, land awkwardly from a jump or rush to net.

Warning signs: Big swelling, bruising, pain on every step, ankle feels unstable or “giving way”.

Immediate care: RICE – Rest, Ice, Compression bandage, Elevation. Do not forcefully stretch it on day one. If you cannot take 4–5 steps without heavy pain, get an X-ray check.

Prevention / future: Strengthen calf and ankle muscles, do single-leg balance drills, replace worn-out shoes and avoid dusty/slippery courts.

Wrist & elbow Neck & upper back
Wrist strain (spin-heavy play)
Overuse

Aching wrist, especially during heavy spin serves or flicks, or after long multi-ball sessions.

Green: settles within a day Details ▼

Common when: Very wristy strokes, sudden increase in spin practice, gripping racket too tight.

Warning signs: Pain with simple daily tasks (typing, writing, holding a glass), swelling around wrist.

Immediate care: Reduce volume of extreme spin serves and flicks, use ice for 10–15 mins after play, rest 1–2 days if pain is sharp.

Prevention / future: Use more forearm & body rotation in strokes, do light wrist strengthening and mobility, avoid very stiff racket setups if you’re still building strength.

Neck & upper back tightness
Posture

Stiffness around neck, shoulders and between shoulder blades after leaning forward for long sessions.

Green: stiffness, not sharp pain Details ▼

Common when: No breaks during practice, head poked forward, always in one stance.

Warning signs: Headaches, tingling into arms, pain that wakes you at night – see a professional.

Immediate care: Gentle stretches, heat pack after training, easy mobility moves for neck and upper back.

Prevention / future: Add basic posture exercises (rows, band pull-aparts), take micro-breaks to stand tall and reset between sets.

100m Sprint
High speed · Explosive load on legs
Hamstrings & calves Shin & Achilles
Hamstring pull
Acute

Sudden pulling pain at the back of the thigh during a sprint, sometimes with a “pop” feeling.

Pink: stop sprinting immediately Details ▼

Common when: No warm-up, sudden max sprint after being still, fatigue at end of session.

Warning signs: Pain when walking fast, running or bending forward, visible bruising after 1–2 days.

Immediate care: Stop sprinting. Use ice in the first 24–48 hours, walk only as comfortable, avoid aggressive stretching early. Serious tears need medical check and rehab plan.

Prevention / future: Always warm up with dynamic drills (A-skips, high knees, leg swings), add hamstring strengthening (bridges, Nordic curls if guided), increase sprint load gradually.

Shin splints (front of leg pain)
Overuse

Aching along the front/inside of the shin bone, especially when you start running or after sessions.

Green: settles with rest days Details ▼

Common when: Big jump in running volume, hard surfaces, worn-out shoes.

Warning signs: Pain in one small spot on shin bone, pain even at rest – might need imaging to rule out stress fracture.

Immediate care: Reduce running volume, switch some sessions to cycling/swimming, ice after sessions, avoid continuing high-impact if pain is increasing.

Prevention / future: Build mileage slowly, use proper running shoes, add calf and shin strengthening, avoid only concrete surfaces.

Achilles tendon irritation
Overuse

Stiffness and pain at the back of the heel, especially first thing in the morning or start of runs.

Green: eases after warm-up Details ▼

Common when: Sudden increase in sprint volume or hill sprints, tight calves, poor recovery.

Warning signs: Pain that gets worse with more running, visible swelling or thickening of the tendon, pain with simple walking.

Immediate care: Reduce high-speed work, avoid uphill sprints, use ice after training, elevate legs.

Prevention / future: Add calf raises (straight and bent knee), regular calf stretching, progress sprint intensity step by step instead of huge jumps.

Football

Knees & ankles Hamstrings & groin
Ankle sprain (tackle / uneven ground)
Acute

Twist or roll of the ankle during tackle, landing or sudden direction change, followed by swelling.

Pink: limp = no more game Details ▼

Common when: Poor studs on grass, uneven ground, late contact or uncontrolled tackle.

Warning signs: Big swelling, ankle feels wobbly, cannot jog without pain – need a proper check.

Immediate care: RICE protocol, no “running it off”. Use crutches if advised; heavy injuries may need immobilisation initially.

Prevention / future: Strengthen around ankle, work on balance, choose correct footwear for surface, warm-up properly before intense play.

Knee strain (overload / jump landings)
Impact / load

Deep ache around knee after lots of running, jumps or playing multiple matches close together.

Green: better after 1–2 easy days Details ▼

Common when: Very high load, weak glutes/thighs, stiff hips/ankles, hard grounds.

Warning signs: Locking, catching, knee giving way, or swelling inside the joint – get a medical check.

Immediate care: Cut down match minutes for a while, use ice after play, simple pain-free range exercises.

Prevention / future: Strengthen quads and glutes (squats, lunges, bridges), practice soft landings with bent knees, vary surfaces if possible.

Hamstring / groin tightness
Overuse

Tight or crampy feeling at the back of thigh or inner thigh during repeated sprints or long halves.

Green: mild, improves with warm-up Details ▼

Common when: Poor warm-up, dehydration, weak posterior chain or adductors.

Warning signs: Sudden sharp pull, cannot continue sprinting, visible bruise later – treat as a strain.

Immediate care: If it shifts from “tight” to “pulled”, stop playing. Use gentle stretching only if it’s mild tightness, not sharp pain.

Prevention / future: Hamstring and groin strengthening (bridges, Nordic variations, adductor side plank), proper hydration and warm-up before matches.

Cricket
Repetitive bowling · Batting twists · Fielding dives
Shoulder & back (bowlers) Elbow & fingers (batting / catching)
Lower back pain (fast bowlers)
Load

Aching or sharp pain in lower back after spells, especially with mixed or twisted bowling action.

Pink: sharp pain = reduce spells Details ▼

Common when: High bowling volume, poor core/glute strength, technique issues, rapid growth phase in teens.

Warning signs: Pain with simple bending, pain that doesn’t ease with rest, night pain – needs medical review to rule out stress fractures.

Immediate care: Reduce number of overs, avoid intense back-bending drills, use ice/heat as guided, gentle pain-free mobility.

Prevention / future: Work with coach on action, add core and hip strength work, avoid back-to-back overload days without recovery.

Throwing / bowling shoulder pain
Overuse

Pain around shoulder after repeated throws or long bowling sessions, especially for fielders and fast bowlers.

Green: mild and post-session only Details ▼

Common when: Lots of high-effort throws from boundary, long spells with poor warm-up.

Warning signs: Clicking, feeling of slipping, weakness or loss of speed – see a sports physio.

Immediate care: Reduce throwing distance/speed for some time, ice after sessions, avoid repeated “maximum effort” throws.

Prevention / future: Build shoulder endurance (band work, Y-T-W exercises), limit throwing load sensibly, never skip warm-up throws.

Finger jams (catching / wicketkeeping)
Impact

Sudden pain, swelling or deformity in fingers after misjudged catches or wicketkeeping takes.

Pink: crooked finger = urgent check Details ▼

Common when: Ball hits unsupported fingers, poor catching technique away from body.

Warning signs: Visible deformity, finger stuck in bent position, severe swelling – may be fracture or dislocation.

Immediate care: Do not pull aggressively. Ice, splint gently in comfortable position, and get medical imaging.

Prevention / future: Drill catching technique regularly, strengthen grip and finger muscles, use proper wicketkeeping gloves for keepers.

Lawn Tennis
Rackets · Repeated serves · Lateral movement
Elbow & shoulder Knees (court load)
Tennis elbow (backhand & grip)
Overuse

Pain on outer elbow with backhands, gripping racquet or lifting things with palm down.

Green: mild, recent increase in play Details ▼

Common when: Stiff arm backhand, wrong grip size, sudden return after a long break.

Warning signs: Pain even at rest, drop in grip strength, pain lasting many weeks – get professional input.

Immediate care: Reduce play volume temporarily, ice massage, avoid pain-heavy strokes for some time.

Prevention / future: Correct grip size, improve technique, include wrist/forearm strengthening to tolerate load better.

Serve-related shoulder pain
Overuse

Pain during or after serves, especially at the top of motion or during follow-through.

Green: only after long session Details ▼

Common when: Many serves in one go, poor scapular/rotator cuff strength, rushed warm-up.

Warning signs: Pain with daily overhead tasks, repeated clicking, feeling of instability or weakness.

Immediate care: Cut serve volume temporarily, keep general movement pain-free, use ice/heat as guided.

Prevention / future: Add shoulder stability and rotator cuff exercises, progress serve count gradually, balance forehand/backhand/serve loads.

Knee load on hard courts
Impact

Aching knees after sessions on hard courts, especially around front of knee or just below kneecap.

Green: settles with 1–2 days rest Details ▼

Common when: Lots of start-stop movement, weak quads/glutes, very stiff ankles/hips.

Warning signs: Swelling, locking, giving way or pain climbing stairs – check with a professional.

Immediate care: Reduce back-to-back hard-court days, ice after play, low-impact cross-training on non-court days.

Prevention / future: Strength train legs and hips, use good quality shoes, mix softer surfaces if available.
