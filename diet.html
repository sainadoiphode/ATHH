Fuel for the way you play

Simple, sport-wise diet ideas for student athletes.

Instead of confusing macros and numbers, this page converts your age group, sport and training days into plain-language suggestions using familiar Indian foods.

What "balanced" roughly means here

Each plan quietly balances three things:

  • Carbs – energy (rice, roti, poha, fruits)
  • Protein – repair (dal, beans, paneer, curd, milk, eggs if used)
  • Good fats – long energy (nuts, seeds, ghee in small amounts)

Sample day – fuel timeline

Think of this as a structure, not a strict menu.

Choose your age, sport and training days on the left.
All day · Hydration
  • Start your day with a glass of water as soon as you wake up.
  • Through the day, keep sipping.
  • During training: small sips every 10–15 min.
Before training
  • 1–2 hours before: light meal with carbs + protein.
  • 30–45 min before: small snack if needed.
After training
  • Within 30–45 min: water + protein + carb.
  • Avoid only junk right after.
Main meals focus
  • Each main meal: 1 carb + 1 protein + veggies.
  • If you eat eggs: include 1–2 on training days.

On rest days, keep meals similar but slightly smaller portions.
